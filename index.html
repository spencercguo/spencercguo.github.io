Spencer Guo

Hi! Welcome to my personal website. My name is Spencer Guo and I'm an undergraduate at Stanford University studying chemistry. This fall, I'll be starting a PhD in chemistry at the University of Chicago.

You can read more about me, my gastronomic experiences, or my contact information by clicking the links.

About

My academic interests lie broadly in theoretical and computational chemistry, including simulation of biochemical systems, quantum mechanics, and statistical mechanics. I currently work in the Markland group investigating various methods of simulation for condensed-phase systems (mostly water). You can read more about that work here! Previously, I worked in the Chen lab synthesizing optochemical probes for studying zebrafish development and at Genentech performing solid-phase peptide synthesis.

Besides science, I play violin in the Stanford Collaborative Orchestra (SCOr). Previously, I also played in the Stanford Symphony Orchestra.

I also love all things food and cooking, whether it be American staples such as mashed potatoes, a classic French bœuf bourguignon, or Shanghainese specialities ranging from drunken hairy crab to shengjianbao. I'm a huge fan of The Food Lab, headed by the inimitable J. Kenji López-Alt, which I credit to giving me a knowledge of the scientific principles which underlie cooking.
